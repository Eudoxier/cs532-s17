    The Public Editor

    Liz Spayd, the sixth public editor, is at the intersection of Times readers and Times journalists. More

    Liz Spayd is the sixth public editor appointed by The New York Times. She evaluates journalistic integrity and examines both the quality of the journalism and the standards being applied across the newsroom. She writes a regular column expressing her views. The public editor works outside of the reporting and editing structure of the newsroom and receives and answers questions or comments from readers and the public, principally about news and other coverage in The Times. Her opinions and conclusions are her own.

     

    Follow the public editor on Twitter @spaydl and reach her by email at public@nytimes.com.
